Stay inside and don't let the dreams out

Kavan TheKid's first ever published photography book!

Synopsis: Stay Inside and Don't Let the Dreams Out is a surreal perspective on the struggle of dealing with depression, internal disarray, and modern societal culture. Each piece is different in its subject matter but the same in its theme; a bleak outlook on the mind and the future of personal communication and connection.
This book will consist of “both metaphorical and literal images that depict my past experiences, emotions and perspectives. It’s a large self-reflection piece that also looks outward at those closest around me.”

All the profits from every book sold will be donated to the Child Mind Institute. The Child Mind Institute is a national non-profit, dedicated to transforming the lives of Children and their families who struggle with Mental Health and Learning Disorders.

About: The book includes every piece from Kavan the Kid’s (Kavan Cardoza) photographic series “Stay Inside & Don’t Let the Dreams Out.” It is told in story form with fifty images spanning over seven chapters.

Size: Standard Portrait, 8×10 in, 20×25 cm


Top2 Works of Kavan TheKid

I Won't Let You Fall

This photo is dedicated to Kavan TheKid's mother.
"It was created with her in mind. It’s a reflection of all the times we feel on the edge, that we were about to fall to our demise and yet, there is someone to lift you up. To save you. The reason I chose to use a crow above any other bird is the crow is always related to death. The crow is wonderful creature and I wanted to display it as the hero. Have it related to life as opposed to death."

What We Take

Another photo that holds a special place to Kavan TheKid.
"It’s essentially the opposite of 'I Won’t Let You Fall.'' The photo is a metaphorical representation of how we live off others or how we take from them for our own benefit. That’s why I chose doves for this image above any other bird. Doves are pure and I feel represent hope, beauty and innocence. To have them flying around this monster-esk character, if the photo were to come alive like a film, the monster would suck the life from each of them."
